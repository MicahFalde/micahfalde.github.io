How to choose: if you want to move fast with minimal ops, building on Planning Center's free API lets you customize at the integration layer (your own dashboards, kiosks, attendance tools) without owning a server or security patching. If you want full data ownership and have a developer, a self-hosted base (ChurchCRM — MIT, or Rock) is the path — accept owning the server and patching. ChurchCRM is also worth standing up as a parallel data-sovereignty experiment. Full detail in docs/chms-comparison.md.

Church Center — Planning Center's member app (the one FBCNP uses)

The congregation-facing app for Planning Center — it is not a standalone product. A church "using Church Center" is running Planning Center behind it, so adopting it is choosing PCO as the data backbone. It's what satisfies R14 (member self-service) and would be the "app" lane in our multi-modal attendance design.

The app itself is free — no setup or per-app fee; you only pay for the PCO modules behind each feature (see the Cost Estimator). There's also a web version (ourchurch.churchcenter.com) so people who won't install an app get the same features in a browser. Trade-offs: it's a container app (members open "Church Center" and pick our church — no custom-branded app store listing), layouts aren't customizable, and every feature assumes the member installs it and has email — which is why the attendance design keeps the app as one lane, never the only lane. No Bible/devotional content — it's logistics, not discipleship. Fits you if: the backbone is Planning Center and you want giving, check-in, groups, and sign-ups in members' pockets at no extra software cost.

No free tool has frictionless live SongSelect import — that's a paid-product feature. FreeShow is the best free PCO+streaming combo (manage lyrics manually); OpenLP is the other free option that talks to PCO.

CCLI is three separate purchases — the licenses are the hard legal requirement (needed the moment you project or print copyrighted lyrics); the software is optional. Licenses are priced by average weekly attendance.

① Copyright License

~$246/yr

The baseline. Project & print lyrics, custom songbooks/arrangements for in-person worship. Unlocks SongSelect. Does not cover online/streaming.

② Streaming License

~$175/yr

Required the moment a worship song appears in a livestream or posted video. Cheaper (~$84) if you already hold the Copyright License. "Plus" adds backing-track rights.

③ SongSelect

~$171/yr

The catalog (230k+ songs) — lyrics, chord charts, lead sheets, transposition. A subscription, not a license; requires the Copyright License. Advanced ~$171 / Premium ~$215.

Professional church email (pastor@ / office@ our-own-domain) needs two things: a domain name and an email platform. Both major platforms are free for verified 501(c)(3) nonprofits — so cost isn't the differentiator; pick by which tools the office will live in. The real timeline driver is nonprofit verification — and email doesn't have to wait for it.

Order of operations

The paperwork is the long pole — everything else is days, not months. Start email on a paid "bridge" and convert to free later; it's a billing change, not a migration.

1Now
Buy the domain (~$12/yr)

Used for both website and email. Registrar account owned by the church (board-accessible credentials), never a volunteer's personal account.

2Now
Put the website live on the domain

Already built — About / Beliefs / Service Times / Contact satisfies Google's "live 5-page site with HTTPS" review bar. No "under construction" text.

3Now
Start bridge email — 2–3 seats (~$15–25/mo)

pastor@ and office@ working immediately on the paid plan. Email does not wait on the 501(c)(3) paperwork.

4Slow
501(c)(3) documentation — the long pole

Churches are auto-exempt but usually not in the IRS database that verifiers (Goodstack for Google; Microsoft's own check) query. As an independent church there's no denominational group exemption to lean on → own EIN + voluntary Form 1023/1023-EZ for a determination letter. Months, not the "2–14 days" often quoted — and it helps well beyond email (donor confidence, vendor discounts).

5Later
Convert to the free nonprofit tier

Same mailboxes, same files, same domain — you just stop paying. Google: conversion form from the Starter plan. Microsoft: swap licenses in the admin center, cancel the paid subscription.

Digital-identity ground rules — whichever platform is picked

AMust
Role-based addresses, not personal names

pastor@, office@, hello@ — when people transition, the successor takes over the inbox and all history/contacts stay intact.

BShould
Aliases for routing, not extra inboxes

CMust
Church-owned ownership
