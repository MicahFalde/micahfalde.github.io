A Country Peach Pie

Fresh peaches under a lattice, in a Crisco crust.

A color clipping she saved — a Crisco advertisement.

Ingredients

  • 2 cpastry flourcrust
  • ⅔ cCriscocrust
  • 1 tsaltcrust
  • —cold watercrust
  • 8–10ripe peachesfilling
  • ¾ cpowdered sugarfilling
  • ½ tlemon juicefilling
  • ⅛ tsaltfilling

Method

  1. Cut the Crisco into the flour and salt with two knives until the consistency of small peas. Add enough cold water to hold together. Divide and roll about ⅛ inch thick, and line a pie plate.
  2. To keep the crust from soaking, brush it with melted Crisco and dust with flour. Peel and slice the peaches into the shell.
  3. Cover with the powdered sugar, then sprinkle with the lemon juice and salt. Dot with bits of Crisco.
  4. Roll the top crust, cover, and bake in a hot oven (450 °F) about 15 minutes; reduce to moderate (350 °F) until done, about 15 minutes more.

As written

A Country Peach Pie
Crust: 2 cups pastry flour · ⅔ cup Crisco · 1 teaspoon salt · cold water
Filling: 8 to 10 ripe peaches · ¾ cup sugar (powdered) · ½ teaspoon lemon juice · ⅛ teaspoon salt

Cut Crisco into flour and salt with two knives until the consistency of small peas. Add enough water to hold together. Divide and roll about ⅛ inch thick. Cover pie plate. To prevent juice soaking in, brush over with melted Crisco and dust with flour. Peel and slice the peaches into the pie plate. Cover with the powdered sugar, then sprinkle with the lemon juice and salt. Dot with bits of Crisco. Roll remaining pie crust, cover and bake in hot oven (450° F.), about 15 minutes. Reduce heat to moderate (350° F.), until pie is done, about 15 minutes more.
