Recipe for 4 Loaves

A big batch of plain white bread — knead it or don’t.

A clipping she saved, pasted to a card.

Ingredients

  • 1 packetquick yeast
  • 1 clukewarm water
  • 3 clukewarm milk, or part water and part milk
  • 4 tsalt
  • 4 tsugar
  • 4 Tfat
  • ~12 cflour

Method

  1. Soften the yeast in the lukewarm water. In a big bowl put the rest of the liquid (scald and cool raw milk to lukewarm), the salt, sugar and softened fat, cutting the fat into small pieces with the spoon.
  2. Add about a pint of flour and blend into a batter. Add the yeast, then the rest of the flour, stirring it in quickly with a spoon.
  3. Turn onto a floured board and knead lightly until smooth, about 7 or 8 minutes. (No-knead method: fold the dough over several times, rest 10 minutes, then work ½ minute.)
  4. Cover and let rise in a greased bowl until nearly double (about 2 hours). Turn out, flatten and fold over 4 times; let rise again until nearly double (about 40 minutes).
  5. Cut into 4 pieces, fold into balls, rest 20 minutes under a bowl. Flatten, stretch, fold and roll into loaves. Grease the tops, let rise until nearly doubled, and bake 45–50 minutes in a moderately hot oven (400 °F).

Recipe for 4 Loaves
1 packet quick yeast · 1 c. lukewarm water · 3 c. lukewarm milk, or part water and part milk · 4 tsp. salt · 4 tsp. sugar · 4 tblsp. fat · About 12 c. flour

Soften yeast in lukewarm water. In a big bowl put the rest of the liquid (if raw milk is used, scald and cool to lukewarm), salt, sugar, and softened fat, cutting it into small pieces with the spoon. Add about a pint of flour, blend into a batter. Add yeast, then rest of flour, stirring it in quickly with a spoon. Turn onto a floured board and knead lightly until smooth, about 7 or 8 minutes. For the no-knead method, fold the dough over several times, let rest 10 minutes, then work ½ minute. Cover and let rise in a greased bowl until nearly double in bulk (about 2 hours in a warm place). Turn onto board, flatten and fold over 4 times. Again let rise until nearly double in bulk (about 40 minutes). Turn onto board, cut into 4 pieces, fold into balls. Let rest 20 minutes, covered by bowl or pans. Flatten out, stretch, fold and roll into loaves. Grease top of loaves, let rise until nearly doubled, bake 45 to 50 minutes in a moderately hot oven (400° F.).
