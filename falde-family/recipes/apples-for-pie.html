Apples for Pie

Apples put up in summer, ready for a winter pie.

A clipping she saved — a reader’s letter to a newspaper “Forum,” signed Mrs. Thompson of St. Paul.

Ingredients

  • —apples, peeled, cored and sliced
  • 1 csugar, per gallon of sliced apples

Method

  1. Peel, core and slice the apples. Place in a crockery jar and, to each gallon of sliced apples, add 1 cup sugar.
  2. Mix well, cover with a plate, and let stand 12 hours.
  3. Pack firmly into sterilized jars to within ½ inch of the top, pressing so the juice covers the apples.
  4. Seal and process 20 minutes in a boiling-water bath. Remove to cool. Add seasoning and more sugar when making the pies.

As written

Apples for Pie
DEAR FORUM: Peel, core and slice apples. Place in crockery jar and to each gallon of sliced apples add 1 cup sugar. Mix well, cover with plate and let stand 12 hours. Pack in sterilized jars firmly to within ½ inch of top, pressing down so that juice covers apples. Seal and process 20 minutes in boiling water bath. Remove from canner to cool. Add seasoning and more sugar to apples when making pies. — Mrs. Thompson, St. Paul.
