Delicious Pickles

A quick vinegar pack, with a dill batch besides.

On a sheet written and signed by Belva Wall.

Ingredients

  • —cucumbers, washed and packed in jars
  • 1 galvinegar
  • 1 csalt
  • ½ pkgdry mustard, worked to a paste with a little vinegar

Method

  1. Wash the cucumbers and pack into sterilized jars.
  2. Make the solution: work the dry mustard to a paste with a little vinegar, then combine with the vinegar and salt. This is enough for about 3 gallons of pickles in glass jars.
  3. Pour over the cucumbers and seal at once.

The sheet carries a second recipe, Dill Pickles: soak about 100 medium cucumbers overnight in salt water (1½ cups salt to 4 quarts water), then pack in jars in layers with dill (and, if you like, grated horseradish and mustard seed) and a small red pepper per jar, cover with a boiled-and-cooled brine, and let ferment 3 or 4 days before sealing.

As written

Delicious Pickles
Wash cucumbers, pack in sterilized jars. Solution (sufficient for 3 gal. of pickles in glass jars): 1 gal. vinegar · 1 cup salt · ½ pkg. dry mustard (work into paste with a little of the vinegar). Pour over cucumbers, seal at once.

Dill Pickles — Place 100 medium-sized cucumbers in salt water overnight, using 1½ cups salt to 4 qts. water. Pack in sterilized jars in layers separated by dill; if you like, add 1 cup grated horseradish and 1 cup mustard seed, and a small red pepper for each jar. Cover with boiled, cooled brine, no air bubbles. These will ferment 3 or 4 days, then seal. — Written by Belva Wall
