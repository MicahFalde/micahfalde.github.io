Salt Pork

A farm brine for curing side pork by the hundredweight.

In her hand, in pencil. The back is an unrelated errand list — flour, salt, margarine; cough syrup, cough drops.

Ingredients

  • —side pork, cut in 5 × 5 pieces
  • —dry salt, to roll the meat in
  • 8 lbssaltbrine, per 100 lbs meat
  • 2 lbsbrown sugarbrine
  • 4 galhot waterbrine

Method

  1. Cut the side pork in 5 × 5 pieces. Roll in dry salt, meat side down. Pack in a crock and set overnight.
  2. For 100 lbs of meat, make the brine: 8 lbs salt, 2 lbs brown sugar and 4 gallons hot water — enough salt that it will float an egg.
  3. Bring the brine to a boil, let it cool, and pour over the meat. Cure 6–8 weeks.

This is a whole-hog farm recipe — a brine measured for 100 pounds of pork. The “float an egg” test is the old way of judging when a brine holds enough salt.
