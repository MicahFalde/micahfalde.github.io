Chiffon Cake

An oil cake, light as air — with an orange version besides.

In pencil, with a “Fluffy Orange Icing” clipping taped to the corner.

Ingredients

  • 2¼ ccake flourmeasure & sift together
  • 1½ csugardry
  • 3 tbaking powderdry
  • 1 tsaltdry
  • ½ ccooking oilmake a well, add in order
  • 5egg yolks, unbeatenwet
  • ¾ ccold waterwet
  • 2 tvanillawet
  • —grated rind of 1 lemonwet
  • 1 cegg whites (7 or 8)whip very stiff
  • ½ tcream of tartarwith the whites

Method

  1. Measure and sift the flour, sugar, baking powder and salt together.
  2. Make a well and add the oil, yolks, water, vanilla and lemon rind in order. Beat with a spoon until smooth.
  3. Whip the egg whites and cream of tartar until very stiff — stiffer than for angel food; do not underbeat.
  4. Pour the yolk mixture gradually over the whipped whites, folding gently with a rubber scraper just until blended. Do not stir. Pour into an ungreased pan at once (a 10-inch tube pan, or a 9 × 13).
  5. Bake 55 minutes at 325 °F, then raise to 350 °F for 10–15 minutes. Invert on two other pans until cold; rap sharply to loosen.

For an orange chiffon, she notes: use ¾ cup orange juice instead of the water. The taped-on Fluffy Orange Icing: cream 1½ 3-oz packages of cream cheese until fluffy, gradually beat in 2¼ cups sifted confectioners’ sugar, then stir in the grated rind of two oranges (1½ tbsp); thin with a few drops of orange juice if needed.

As written

“Chiffon” cake
2¼ cups cake flour · 1½ cups sugar · 3 tsp. baking powder · 1 tsp. salt (measure and sift, handle lightly)
½ cup cooking oil · 5 unbeaten egg yolks · ¾ cup cold water · 2 tsp. vanilla · grated rind of 1 lemon (make a “well” and add in order, beat with spoon until smooth)
1 cup egg whites (7 or 8) · ½ tsp cream of tartar (whip until very stiff, stiffer than for angel food, do not underbeat)

Pour egg yolk mixture gradually over whipped egg whites, gently folding with rubber scraper just until blended. Do not stir. Pour into ungreased pan immediately (10 in. pan, 4 in. deep. 9×13 fits in too). Bake 55 min. 325°, then increase to 350° for 10–15 min. Turn upside down on 2 other pans until cold. Hit sharply to loosen.
Orange chiffon — use ¾ orange juice instead of water.
