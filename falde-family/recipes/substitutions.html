Substitutions

What to reach for when the pantry comes up short.

A clipping she saved, signed J. B. — a little table of baking swaps.

Method

  1. Use ⅞ cup water instead of 1 cup milk; add 2 teaspoons shortening.
  2. Use 1⅓ cups brown sugar instead of 1 cup white sugar.
  3. Use 1 cup sour milk instead of 1 cup sweet milk; add ½ teaspoon soda and subtract 1½ teaspoons baking powder.
  4. Use ¾ to ⅚ cup whole-wheat or graham flour instead of 1 cup white flour (best if at least half the flour stays white).
  5. Use ¼ cup cocoa instead of 1 square of chocolate; add 1½ teaspoons shortening.

Not a recipe — a reference she kept in the box for the days an ingredient ran out.

As written

It's a good thing to know how to make substitutions in a recipe — whether to create a new flavor, to use old supplies, or to make up for a missing ingredient.

Use ⅞ cup of water instead of 1 cup of milk. Add 2 teaspoons shortening. · Use 1⅓ cups of brown sugar instead of 1 cup of white sugar. · Use 1 cup of sour milk instead of 1 cup of sweet milk. Add ½ teaspoon soda and subtract 1½ teaspoons baking powder. · Use ¾ to ⅚ cup of whole-wheat or graham flour instead of a cup of white flour. Results are best if at least half the flour in the final recipe is white flour. · Use ¼ cup cocoa instead of 1 square of chocolate. Add 1½ teaspoons of shortening. — J.B.
